As the vampire formerly known as Lambert attempts to flee, Fax looses an arrow, striking the fleeing creature and causing it to fall back to the ship. A battle ensues, and the Works in Progress quickly dispatch the creature. After doing so, they check the rest of their shipmates for any bites and are satisified that no one has been bitten. After planning on what their next steps are, they confront Captain Bird to inform him that they are having hesitations about his request to rob a bank in Borbir. A tense verbal exchange occurrs, in which Bird is attempting to get money from the party for ferrying them to Borbir, while the party believes they paid their fair share by keeping the crew alive after two attacks. Eventually, Bird accepts a net gain of 50 gold pieces after agreeing to pay his "bodyguards." Eventually, in the evening, the ship lands in Borbir, a city built atop the cliffs of western Kalinar. Before the party sets out, Bird apologizes for his behavior the night before, noting that he had a long talk with his son, Galen, about their future. Bird notes that he has been convinced that piracy is not the life he wants to live any longer and will begin a legitimate business of ferrying people throughout western Kalinar on his ship. As a token of his gratitude for the group's efforts to keep them alive and as an apology, he gives the party old cloaks that he does not realize are old Justicar Cloaks of Protection. The party accepts and begins to make their way up the cliffside stairway into the city proper, where they encounter a courtyard with a mime performing in a crowd of people. Eventually the mime approaches and attempts to distract them while an apparent accomplice attempts to steal gold from Zos. Shayna sees this attempt, grabs hold of the creature's hand, which belongs to a female dwarf, and after atempts to get the mime to confess, the mime pretends that he does not know who the dwarf is and makes his way off, the show apparently ending abruptly.
