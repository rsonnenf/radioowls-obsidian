After defeating the druids, you questioned [[Davyd Burnside|Mayor Burnside]] and First Lady [[Alina Fox]] about what has been happening. Alina told you that, after you dealt with the Dire Clan attacks, not long afterwards, the town started to be targeted by a group of rogue druids. Eventualy, the attacks grew larger, and tonight's attack was the largest yet. She also noted that much of the town is beginning to question the leadership abilities of her and her husband. They noted that a name kept being said in reverence during the attack: [[Aralus]]. During the conversation, [[Grubby]] convinced the mayor to allow him to take his valuable elephant on the nightstand. Fox, on the other hand, was turned off by [[Barnaby Baffletop|Barnaby]]'s attempts to obtain reimbursement for using his revivify spell on the Mayor. She allowed you to stay in a room in the southern part of town for the evening, though, and you all stayed there fro the night. During the evening, [[Bo Fortuna|Bo]] and Barnaby interacted a bit. Bo watched Barnaby as Barnaby created a small deer, intending to imbue the deer with the soul he had taken. Unnerved by Bo's watching him, though, Barnaby gave up the soul he had taken, and Bo ended up drinking the soul. Eventually they went to bed, and after the rest of [[the Works in Progress]] took their watches, they woke up and went to [[Ilga]] to see if he had any potions he could give them. He did not, but after hearing Barnaby mention "Potion of Dragon Majesty," he pulled out a tome and gave Grubby a page that noted that the potions *can* be made permanent if an [[Orbs of Dragonkind|Orb of Dragonkind]] and other, unknown ingredients are used. He told the group that Heartwood is hidden in the Misty Forest and could help them if they sought him out and found him. The group left and traveled through the forest. During their travels, Barnaby noted that he was intending to imbue a costruct with a soul, and the party talked about the ethics of doing that and breaking rules and laws. Eventually, they came across a camp where they saw ornate tents and individuals in druidic clothing. They were talking about what targets to attack next, and the apparent leader of the group called out for a "[[Zif]]," to prepare himself for the next strike.
