Making a hasty escape from [[Bilell]], the group meets on a boat where they meet Captain Bird and his son, Galen. They ship off out into the Bay of Bilell, where they will eventually make their way to the open seas. Captain Bird, clearly a pirate, runs his ship, the Forgetful Lass, with Galen, who clearly wants little to do with his father's exploits in piracy. Clearly, Bird, in turn, wants little to do with his son, whom he perceives as weak and not cut out to be a pirate. As a result, Bird takes a liking to [[The Works In Progress]], having seen how they dispatched the Bilell guards on the dock. He allows them to get comfortable in the cargo hold below deck, and you all make your way there where you have a moment to debrief about what has happened. [[Barnaby Baffletop|Barnaby]] conveys his anger and disappointment with Grubby and Fax for leaving without letting the group know where they were headed, placing the completion of the contract in jeopardy. Grubby explains that he was motivated to dispatch Councilwoman Forrest because he was tired of running away, but realized that running away was causing many of his problems. He resolved not to run away anymore, including his friends. Fax explained that his actions were irrationally motivated by his fear of losing someone like he lost his wife and son to Emperor Fen's cruelty. With the group detailing their pasts, Zos and Barnaby decided to contribute as well. Zos explained that she grew up with a bugbear named [[Zif]] who taught her how to live in the wilderness and encouraged her to live, above all, by the rule of "do no harm." She explained that, one day, [[Zif]] left her, apparently to go to town, and he never returned. Although she waited for a long time, she went to a nearby town, herself to find him, which led to her finding Barnaby and Bo. Finally Barnaby volunteered that he grew up in the mountains outside of Courdney, for much of his life without his mother, who left 25 years ago. She went on, apparently, to become a skilled artificer, but she did not make her family a priority. He explained that he is motivated by getting jobs done and working with [[The Works In Progress]] as work colleagues first, not as friends first. As the group probed this, Bird met them and told them that, if he was to ferry them, he required payment. He said his form of payment would come in the form of the party performing a heist for him. They would break into a vault in Borbir holding a vast sum of money and return it to him, and he seemed to suggest that they would be in danger if they did not. Barnaby was adamantly opposed to the idea of committing a crime, but others in the group were not as opposed.
