You agreed to take on Mayari's quest to free Bahamut and Grummsh. She told you that [[Barnaby Baffletop|Barnaby]]'s mother is currently located in Hove. Before you left the underrealm, she gave you the magic items you asked for. You then entered the portal and were transported back to the beach. You went back to [[Bilell]] and stopped at the tavern to drop off Liris. [[Deekek]] was thrilled to see her partner back. He told you that you could go to the [[Red Thorn]] for information. He told you that a woman named [[Rilad]] recently became head of this organization. Eventually, you went shopping and picked up a few things before heading back to the Artificer's Guild. You convinced [[Feril]] to take you to the Frozen Pinewood in his airship the following morning.

Taking off

You watch as Feril gives a few last-minute instructions to the artificers remaining at the guild. Before long, he picks up a large bag from behind the front desk, one that he obviously packed the night before. He walks toward the front of the guild hall and opens the door. He stops before crossing the threshold, basking in the sunlight and taking a deep breath, preparing himself for the adventure to come. He exhales, turns to you, and gives you all a smile. Whether to reassure you or reassure himself, you're not sure. At any rate, he leads you outside and around the back of the guild hall. In the back, there is a vast open bare space--nothing sits on top of it. No tables or chairs, which is curious, considering this is a wonderful looking massive courtyard that would be great for recreation. Feril, though, leads you toward a set of stairs next to the courtyard, leading you deep underground, under the courtyard. And it's there that everything becomes clear. You enter a massive area. The ceiling is a few hundred feet high, and you initially think you've entered a garage. That is, until you see the massive ship in front of you. Its body looks like a boat. But above it appears to be a blimp-like structure. The ship is levitating, and you discern that it is due to some sort of magic. At that point, Feril calls out, "Open the doors!" and you realize why nothing was set up on the courtyard above you. It wasn't a courtyard, but rather, retractable doors that will allow the ship to levitate out of. Light pours into the hangar, and Feril looks to you all with a proud look on his face. "Well, time to get aboard. This is my baby's maiden voyage, and we're both incredibly excited to get going." You climb aboard the ship, which is pristine. The deck is made of beautiful wood. The steering apparatus stands on a large platform, and Feril takes his spot there. He grabs hold of a knob and twists a few gears before, with barely a sound, the ship begins to lift higher into the air and out through the space, now opened, at the top of the massive room. As the ship makes it to ground level, you hear gasps and shouts in awe, no doubt flabbergasted and amazed at the sight of the ship. Feril, however, pays them no mind, concentrating diligently on the ship, guiding it high into the air, and before long, the city of Bilell is below you. A hundred feet. Hundreds of feet. A thousand? You take a look out toward the horizon behind you. You see the shores of the continent of Kalinar and the vast ocean. You are struck at how large the world is and how much more you have to see. You turn back toward the bow of the ship and look ahead of you. Vast greens of plains and forests are below you as you climb higher and higher, just above some of the lower-hanging clouds. At that point, Feril, who has been concentrating relentlessly until this point, lets out a shout of joy. He looks to his left and right, then to all of you and yells out, "We did it! It worked! This is going to revolutionize travel throughout the world!"
