Three years following the monumental defeat of Emperor Fen at the hands of several mysterious individuals, who have since been determined to be members of the long lost Order of the [[Justicars]], the continent of Kalinar has, generally, experienced some degree of peace.  However, while the continent rebuilds, relieved, from the cruelty of Emperor Fen's reign, a startling number of conflicts continue to serve as blotches on a realm otherwise thankful for the transition of power to the new Emperor [[Christopher]].  As this newly crowned Emperor rebuilds his realm, he, having lived an otherwise politically-inexperienced life, faces challenges unlike any he could have dreamed of facing.  Interterritorial conflict rages in parts of the realm challenging the legitimacy of his rule.  And while he and his Justicars, who themselves are facing their own challenge of rebuilding their Order, once thought extinct, attempt to restore peace and justice throughout the realm, insubordinate territories view this time as an opportunity to grow their own individual power in the political sphere.  Criminal syndicates, in turn, are taking advantage of the instability to strengthen their influence in the seedier parts of the realm.  And to add to this instability, held in check by the slimmest of threads, rumors of supernatural occurrences are becoming more and more common in the realm.  Whispers that a Dark Being revealed himself in the Throne Room at the Battle of Theel lead to widespread concern that the barrier between this world and that of another ethereal plane is becoming thinner and thinner.  More citizens hear tales of demons and grotesque creatures materializing throughout the realm, and they fear that their gods are abandoning them to this chaos.  Only time will tell if that proves to be true.

At the moment, however, [[Barnaby Baffletop|Barnaby]] Baffletop, Bo Fortuna, [[Shayna Thosva|Shayna]] Thosva, and Zos aren't worried by any of that nonsense because, as far as they're concerned, that's a problem for the political elites who are disinterested in the wellbeing of hard-working, everyday people.  You're more than content right here in Varnithel, where you have arrived for the evening after making the decision to come here looking for work.  The four of you have met under chaotic circumstances -- somehow, everything from vampires, to Artificer's Guild contracts, to disappearances of individuals a few of you know, to absolute random chance brought you together.  Maybe it was this sense that things tended to happen between the members of this group that led you all to believe that, by sticking together,  you'd surely find interesting work. Whatever the reason, you find yourself in Varnithel, a village located just on the edge of a lush forest, and the town is a welcome sight. 

After days of trekking through the forest in alternating rainy states of misty to downpour, you are thrilled to get inside and warm up by a fire, especially since, today, the weather falls into the category of "dreary and miserable."  As you walk into town, you find Varnithel to be a small but bustling village.  As the mist continues to come down, you do your best to dodge muddy puddles that are pooling on the streets.  Looking around, you see that although the day is incredibly dreary, it has done little to dampen the spirits of the unusual number of citizens outside.  In fact, as you look around, you notice that the streets are draped with banners, food vendors line the streets, and laughing, inebriated citizens walk the streets with smiles on their faces.  Very clearly, something is happening here in Varnithel.



At Performance

As you make your way to the forest clearing to the north, the rain, thankfully, begins to stop, although a gray fog lingers.  You make your way through the woods, down a well-trodden pathway until you reach the clearing.  In the clearing is a large festival setup.  You pass by tents that house all manner of games, wares, foods, and drinks.  A little further ahead of you is a massive pyre which lights the area.  And beyond that is a beautiful outdoor stage lit by lanterns.   Bales of hay act as seats near the stage. While Grubby and Fax ready themselves for their act, the rest of you find spots on the haybales near the stage, and after a few minutes, the a human male wearing a formal tunic walks onto the stage.  The crowd claps as he walks toward the center, and he announces, "Welcome, everyone, to the final night of the Festival of Lights!  We have four acts for you to close out the festival, which you are sure to enjoy.  Before we begin, I would like to thank the organizers of the Festival and this show, to our vendors and participants, and most importantly, to [[Davyd Burnside|Mayor Burnside]] and his wife, the First Lady, Alina Fox."  He gestures toward a middle aged male human sitting in the front row who is tall and has wild, unkempt hair.  Sitting next to him is a woman with brown hair in functional, but well-kept attire suitable for comfort despite being outdoors in the damp weather all day.  Upon hearing his name, Mayor Burnside, with what appears to be a permanent scowl on his face, simply raises his hand in acknowledgement, but does nothing more.  "Mayor Burnside's unwavering support ensures that this festival is successful every year, and we thank you for your efforts. Beyond that, this town owes its growth and security to your leadership."  Burnside simply nods to the announcer, and a very brief, awkward silence occurs before the announcer calls the first act to the stage. The first act is an elvish man with short, brown hair and pale skin.  He walks onto the stage with a  stringed instrument and begins to pluck at the strings and sing.  The crowd is enraptured by his performance; you watch in silence as the light from the lanterns causes the shadows on the stage to dance, as if to the elf's playing.  After a few songs, the elf concludes, and the crowd, impressed, applauds him as he walks off stage.  Next up is a dark-skinned human female.  She walks onto stage wearing flowing garments that are a deep wine-colored red.  She stops at the center of the stage and stands with her eyes closed for a brief moment.  She then begins to move as a lute begins to play from off on the side of the stage, and she launches into an intricate, yet precise dance routine.  She darts from one part of the stage to the other, peppering in twirls and jumps.  Her routine goes for several minutes until, at its conclusion, the mesmerized crowd claps loudly and cheers.  When she leaves the stage, Grubby and Fax, you learn that it's your turn as an announcer calls you before the crowd.

 . . .

The crowd claps and cheers enthusiastically at the end of the act, and Grubby and Fax exit the stage to whoops and shouts of joy.  Once you're off stage, you hear the announcer name the last act of the evening.  A woman walks onto the stage with a long, loose-fitting tunic, but with sleeves that are very large, as if inflated.  As she approaches the center of the stage, she pauses, then begins an acrobatic martial arts routine.  Over the course of her routine, she engages in several difficult-looking flips and displays her unarmed proficiency, kicking high into the air and falling into several strong-looking stances, which evoke thoughts of immovable boulders or serpents ready to strike.  She quickly moves her left hand forward in a powerful thrust and shouts as she does so. She then does the same with her right, thrusting her hand forward, but Zos, you notice that when she does so, a tiny crossbow appears in her hand from the inside of her large sleeve.
