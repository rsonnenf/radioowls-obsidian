Taking some time to recover after defeating [[the T'Durn]], [[Fax Faertrin|Fax]] goes into the main tent. It appears to be a command center. He takes the map, and [[Grubby]], looking around, finds a Quarterstaff of All Sight. [[Barnaby Baffletop|Barnaby]] prays to Mayari, hoping to contact her, and he is successful. [[Mayari]] is exasperated. She tells [[the Works in Progress]] that Grummsh is volatile. He learned that there is a portal under Den Fatum that will lead to the portion of the Underrealm where he is being held captive, but the fact that she knows this means that Grummsh's rage and anger must be growing to the point that the chains holding him are weakening him. She explains that Grummsh's alliance with her and the other gods opposing the Blight has been a tenuous one. He joined them only because he enjoys warfare, and if his rage causes him to snap, there is no telling whether he will remain an ally. She explains that there is likely still some time before that danger manifest, but it is far less than she thought. With this information, you begin to decide your next step. Zif tells [[Zos]] that he is incredibly proud of his found daughter and all she has accomplished. He suggests that saving the Misty Forest--her home--would bring greater balance to Kalinar. After some concern regarding the time you have to address the situation with Grummsh, you decide that finding [[Heartwood]] and purifying the forest while you are here will not be too distracting. Barnaby tells [[Zif]] to go to Theel to meet with Craigug and [[Feril]] for safety and gives him a note to give to them, which says that they should contact them so that they can be picked up in the Misty Forest. Zif tells Zos how proud he is of her one more time and promises that they will see each other again before shapeshifting into a bird and flying toward Theel. The rest of you made your way to Heartwood. Eventually, you found him deep in the forest. Grubby says that you all are here looking for a way to purify the forest in an attempt to stem the Blight, and part of the way they were hoping to do so was to make a permanent potion of Dragon Majesty. Upon hearing the word "dragon," Heartwood slams his fists into the ground in rage.

## Notes

Heartwood sounds like Treebeard.


If party asks Heartwood to help them concoct a potion of dragon majesty, he becomes angry.  

>*"What business do the likes of you have with such a potion? Do you wish to sully this Forest even more than the Blight already has?"*

Heartwood will explain that, while dragons are relatively rare in Kalinar now, they once roamed throughout Kalinar. Some had little regard for the forest. Great battles between dragons and those who sought to steal from their vast hoards caused significant collateral damage to the Forest. 

>*"All manner of dragon was guilty. Their acid poisoned our mothers and fathers. Their lightning struck down our brothers and sisters. Their flames burned our saplings alive. You aim to facilitate our suffering further?"*

If party is persuasive, Heartwood will tell them that he will consider assisting them if they purify the Misty Forest.

>*"There is a darkness in the forest. Whispers suggest that a minor god has settled in a cave to the northwest. From there, his poison spreads. All manner of dark beings erupt from cracks in the soil and boil into existence from pools of darkness. I hear that two contemptable dragons have allied themselves with   this god. They protect him while he calls forth his minions, which pour into this realm. Rid the forest of this darkness, and I will consider your request."*

After party reaches cave:

>*A cool, stale air wafts out of the cave, but all you can see is darkness inside. Taking another step closer, you begin to hear the tinkling of tiny bells and a deep, dark laugh. "So. You are the mortals that defeated Dranech and Fulad-Zereh." From deep inside the cave, you see a flame spark into existence. "You will not stop the tide. Our army will overrun this realm." At that moment, two dark winged creatures fly out of the cave, letting out ear-piercing screeches, and two red roiling pits emerge from the ground.*
