## Summary of Last Session
---
 The battle raged on! [[Fax Faertrin|Fax]] brought [[Bo Fortuna|Bo]] to unconsciousness multiple times, but [[Barnaby Baffletop|Barnaby]] was determined to keep him up. He used several healing spells to keep Bo up, but the attacks kept coming from Fax, who was under the control of Titivulus. Over the course of the battle, Titivulus's minions were able to break [[Gruumsh]] free of his bonds while he was enraged. Argentius kept Gruumsh's attention while you all focused on Titivulus. However, while that occurred, Fax took the opportunity to fly toward Bo and stab him several times. While Bo clung to life by a thread, Eldird finished off Titivulus with a Magic Missile spell. While Grubby was fleeing, he called for Mayari, hoping that she might be able to help. With Gruumsh now free of his bonds, and Mayari now able to discern his location, she appeared in the center of the chamber. She pleaded with Gruumsh to come back to her and aid them against the Blight, but [[The Works In Progress]] freed Gruumsh too late. He was too enraged to comply. Mayari, now knowing that Leaving Gruumsh to roam free while enraged, made the difficult decision to destroy him to prevent him from causing irreparable destruction in Kalinar. As this happened, Barnaby ran to Bo's side and attempted to resurrect him with magic. As he did so, he and Zos, with all of their might, put their heart and soul into bringing Bo back to life. Fax, too shocked by what he had done, was paralyzed by what he had seen to assist. Despite the efforts of Barnaby and Zos, Bo succumbed to his wounds. His last sense of being was his being awakened in his darkened childhood bedroom, where his patron, a dark god, chastised him for thinking he could go back to Kalinar to be and do good. Bo, satisfied with the goodness he brought into the world, albeit small and only for a short time, broke his connection to his dark patron for good, engulfing him in white light. Satisfied that he was free, Bo returned to his childhood bed where he fell into the deepest, most peaceful sleep he could have ever hoped for.
