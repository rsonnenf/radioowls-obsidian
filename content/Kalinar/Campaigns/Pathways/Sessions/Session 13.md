Continuing along your journey in the cavern, you hear pained yells from a nearby room that grow louder with each step closer to come to it. An individual inside tells you that the closer you come toward the room, the more an enchanted torture device tightens. After a few bungled attempts, you realize that sending Eric, who flies to the room, does not trigger the magical properties of the torture device. Eric frees the gnome, who you learn is named Elvir. He explains that he has been trapped here after approaching the same gem on the beach you all did. As you tend to him, you notice a gem, like the one on the beach, embedded in the wall here. After learning that it feeds off of magical energies, you cast several spells. It harnesses the magical power, and through it, you begin to speak with the apparent ruler of this realm, [[Fulad-Zereh]]. He informs you that you have interrupted his plans and that of his kin and that both you and your realm are doomed. After you ask for a way out, he directs you, via a flaming tendril, toward the north end of the cavern. You make your way there and come across Liris. Initially crazed, you cause her to come to, and she joins your party. Continuing to follow the flaming tendril, you see that it terminates at a door with no handle with a riddle engraved upon it. After discerning that it seeks blood, you give it some. Through the door, which opens before you, comes a massive skeleton wrapped in vessels of viscous blood, like a tree covered in crimson vines. It blocks your way.

Upon entering Final Chamber

You step into the roiling mass and instantly feel an immense pressure on your chests as you are flung into another destination. After a few seconds of agony and motion, you all succumb to stillness fall into a heap on the ground. The same orange lights surround you. The smell of sulphur fills your nostrils. Jagged rocks dig into your palms as you try and push yourselves back onto your feet. You find that you are in a massive chamber. In the distance, you see another roiling mass. Likely another portal. That must be the only way in or out of this chamber because you see no other way to exit. You take a moment to get a better look at your surroundings and are soon startled. The pillars strewn about the room may appear to be uninteresting formations at first glance, but upon closer inspection, they appear to be ornate and purposefully placed. Statutes stand on either side of the walkway you have landed on. This is no ordinary cavern. This is a throne room. Just as you make this connection, you hear a booming, ominous voice announce, "You have entered my domain of the Underrealm uninvited. And you have interrupted the efforts of my kin and me. Who are you to challenge us? To stand against that which is inevitable--the desecration of your realm?"

***

A roiling mass of dark red--some combination of flame and darkness begins to swirl before you. "You are but an insect under my feet. A blemish on the perfection that the Blight works toward. And a twig against the fires of our might. Embrace your doom." A massive creature with wings that, when spread out, block the blinding light of the lava before you. His large horns, yellow eyes, and pointed teeth are intimidating--made even more so by the large axe he carries. He takes the axe into his hand and lifts it above his head in anticipation of the coming battle.

***

The winged creature falls to the ground letting loose and pained cry of disbelief and fear. From the point of injury, you see skin, armor, and weapons begin to collapse inward on itself. The creature looks to you in terror, and watching as he yells in fear and pain, he claws at his wound as his body contorts and pulls itself inward toward a single point until, for a brief moment, all that is left of him is a single speck of darkness in the air. You watch in fascination at the singularity before a blinding flash of light, deafening boom, and powerful outward force originating from that point pushes you to the ground. Almost as immediately as the boom reaches your ears, you are greeted with a deafening silence--that is, except for the bubbling lava around you. You look up, trying to get your bearings and see that the roiling mass before is still intact. In addition to that mass, though, you see two blinding points of light on a pillar just to the right of that mass. Evntually, as your eyes struggle to adjust to the brightness, you see the figures of a woman and a dog. While the light continues to  be blinding, you can make out subtle details of the figures. The woman is dark skinned with flowing hair. She wears a headband across her forehead and bears intricate tattoos across her neck and arms. The dog, in turn, bears wings and stands stoically.

Mayari, goddess of the moon, will be thankful and impressed with the adventurers. She will tell them that she and Kaibigan were captured by Fulad-Zereh for their opposition to his and the rest of the Blight's plans to cross over to the overrealm, take control of it, and rule it. She will explain that she and a select few other gods have attempted to contest the Blight's efforts. But reluctance on the part of many other gods has left her and the other leaders contesting the Blight weak, which led to her capture and to the capture of two other gods. Seeing as the adventurers were able to hold their own and defeat a powerful Blighted god like Fulad-Zereh, she will ask them to free the remaining gods in an effort to protect the overrealm. [[Shayna Thosva|Shayna]]'s necklace will be glowing an immense red. Mayari will recognize this object as somehow related to Gruumsh, one of the other gods leading the charge against the Blight. She will be thankful and reward the party with an answer to one question. IF GRUBBY ASKS IF HE WILL TURN INTO A DRAGON, ANSWER "NO." The answer is he was always a dragon in heart and soul, and his physical appearance merely needed to reflect that.

Asks you to save Grummsh (he seeks to deplatform gods and goddesses who seek to become more victorious than him) and Bahamut. Bahamut is imprisoned in Den Fatum; Grummsh, somewhere in the Frozen Pinewood.
