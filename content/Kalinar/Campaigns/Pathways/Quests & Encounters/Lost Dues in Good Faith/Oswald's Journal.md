The journal reads:

>This afternoon I walked the streets and felt an unusual spring in my step. I cannot be sure if it is the fine weather or something else has gifted me this buoyancy of spirit, but I am grateful nonetheless. I have seen no sign of the girl, thank goodness. Gods willing I have slipped her net at last.  The shadows are strange here, seeming to move of their own accord. As I passed over the streets this morning, I could have sworn I saw one turn to face me, then another. I fled the area as quickly as I could, and as far as I can tell, I was not followed. I have many enemies - as any man of a rogueish, adventurous mien should - though none have cause to search for me here. I know these shadows must be but a trick of the mind, but still I am uneasy.

The next entry is written in a shaky, wild hand, clearly penned in great haste:

>As I put me down to bed this evening, I heard a faint whispering beneath my window. I always leave it open to better catch the breeze, and thanks be for it, or I might have been none the wiser. However, when I went to investigate, no one was there. And yet I know, I KNOW I heard voices. I may be old, perhaps even paranoid, but I am NOT crazy. I must leave this place with all haste. Gods protect me in my flight.

On the next and final page of the journal is a Bear's footprint encircled by bloodied antlers in a deep red ink.
