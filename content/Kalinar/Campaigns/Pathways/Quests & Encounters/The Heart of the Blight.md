## Overview
---
The party stands at the precipice of a climactic battle atop the mountain where the portal to the underrealm churns with dark energy. To destroy the portal from within, they must enter it, navigate the treacherous underrealm, and dismantle the arcane mechanisms that sustain the portal's existence.

## Encounters
---
#### Entering the Portal

As the party approaches the swirling vortex, they must contend with the portal's guardians—Blighted creatures that seek to prevent any interference. The guardians could be a mix of demons and dark gods' minions, such as Shadow Demons (CR 4) and Barlguras (CR 5) from the Monster Manual.

#### Skill Checks:

- **Arcana Check (DC 18)**: To understand the portal's operation and safely enter without being torn apart by its energies.
- **Constitution Saving Throw (DC 15)**: To resist the corrupting influence of the Blight as they pass through the portal.

#### The Underrealm

Once inside, the party finds themselves in an open expanse, rivers of lava travel under a dilapidated bridge. Atop the bridge are blighted creatures making their way to the portal and to Kalinar. The environment is hostile, with geysers of necrotic energy, unstable ground, and the constant threat of Blighted creatures.

* To the west is a lavafall.
* To the south, the fields give way to an even more desolate field.
* Even further south of the fields stands a tall, menacing structure with spires and a ghostly dark mist enveloping it.

#### Environmental Hazards:

- **Necrotic Geysers**: Sudden eruptions that require a DC 17 Dexterity saving throw to avoid necrotic damage.
- **Unstable Terrain**: Crumbling paths over chasms that require DC 16 Acrobatics checks to cross safely.

#### Traps:

- **Soul Siphon**: An arcane trap that drains life force, requiring a DC 19 Wisdom saving throw to resist or suffer exhaustion levels.

#### The Portal's Hearts

Deeper within the underrealm lie the hearts of the portal—massive crystals pulsating with dark energy, tethering the Underrealm to the fabric of the Overrealm.

* Entrance Heart - "We cannot be blemished." The gem cannot be harmed by ranged or magical means.
* Lavafall Heart - "We cannot be tamed." The gem will attack using [[RPG Rules/5e SRD/spells/Finger of Death]]
* Throne Room Gem - "We cannot be stopped." [[RPG Rules/5e SRD/spells/Command|Command]]

#### The Collapse

>*Upon destroying Dregath, a pillar of flame erupts where his body lies. In the heart of that pillar, you see a glint of red, and as the heat dissipates, you see a red gem similar to those you encountered previously. But this one is flawless, beautiful even. Dregath's personal anchor to Kalinar. You hear it call out to you, and immediately you recognize that this gem carries within it the power to do anything you could ever desire. To bring about peace throughout the realm. To protect Kalinar from whatever threat it might encounter in the future....... To make those who would harm Kalinar, your friends, or you pay. With their lives. To take vengeance upon those who wronged you. To kill gods. To rule Kalinar for yourself. You hear a voice inside your head. A voice from the gem. "Take me. I am yours. Use me. To bring goodness to the world. To take what you deserve. To rule as you deserve."*

If the party decides to destroy the gem, attempt a DC 20 Wisdom saving throw. On a failure, the gem retaliates against the defiance it senses from the players, blasting them with 8d10 psychic damage. 

If the party decides to take the gem, the party holding it is bound to do the gem's will, which is to seek power and take over Kalinar as its master wanted at all costs. Attempts to defy it require a DC 20 Wisdom Saving throw. On a failure, the gem retaliates, dealing 8d10 psychic damage.

After the gem is taken or destroyed, the underrealm begins to collapse. The party must escape back through the portal before it closes, racing against time and the crumbling reality around them.

While the party is leaving, roll a die. On a 10 or higher, randomly pick one person to make a DC 20 check to avoid a burst of lava from the ground or falling debris. On a failure, the player takes 3d8 points of fire or physical damage.
#### Conclusion

>*A colossal tremor rips through the mountain, a deafening boom echoing across the battlefield. The Blight portal, a swirling vortex of sickly purple energy that has been spewing forth nightmares threatening Kalinar appears to flicker. Then, with a blinding flash that momentarily drowns out the battle before you, the portal implodes. The unnatural energies that have pulsed through it for so long seem to suck inwards on themselves, collapsing into a tattered void before bursting out before you. A black-purple force engulfs the battlefield from the point of that void, and you see blighted  creatures turn to dust before you, screaming in agony as they succumb to nothingness. The aftershock of the destruction tears into what is left of the mountain. Cracks snake across its surface, glowing purple, before widening into chasms and dimming. What was left of the once majestic peak crumbles, massive chunks shearing away and tumbling downwards. Dust and debris choke the air, a swirling brown shroud that swallows the battlefield whole.*


If the party succeeds in destroying the portal's heart and escaping, they emerge atop the mountain to a world saved from the Blight's invasion. Their victory is celebrated, and they are hailed as heroes of Kalinar.
