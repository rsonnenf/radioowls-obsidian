## Overview
---
After crash landing in the [[The Forgotten Sands|Forgotten Sands]], the party regroups and makes their way through the desert. Eventually, they inadvertently make their way into the territory of a desert sphinx. The sphinx challenges them to solve three riddles or be killed.

## Encounters
---
>*As the sun casts long shadows across the shifting dunes of the desert, the Works in Progress finds themselves facing an unexpected sight. Ahead, amidst the golden sands, stands a majestic and enigmatic figure—a sphinx with the head of a vulture and the body of a lion. Its presence exudes an aura of ancient wisdom and a hint of solemnity.
>As the party approaches, the sphinx's keen vulture eyes fixate on them. It speaks with a voice that carries the weight of centuries, "Travelers of the desert, seekers of truths, you stand before me at a crossroads. To proceed, you must first prove your worthiness."
>With a graceful stretch of its wings, the sphinx rises, a creature of both awe-inspiring and unsettling beauty. Beside it stand two imposing ogres, their expressions alert and expectant.
>"Answer me three riddles, and you shall earn your passage. Fail, and the guardians at my side shall become your foes," the sphinx declares, its gaze unyielding yet not devoid of a glimmer of curiosity, as if it anticipates the outcome with a patience that only eons can grant.*

Riddle 1:  **What word is pronounced the same if you take away four of its five letters?** Answer: Queue.

Riddle 2: **What do you bury when it's alive and dig up when it's dead? Answer: A plant.

Riddle 3: **What do you lose the moment you share it?**  Answer: A secret.

### If the Party Solves All Riddles 
>*A triumphant gleam lights up the vulture eyes of the sphinx as the last riddle's answer leaves your lips. With each correct response, its aura seems to shift, a combination of approval and a deeper understanding. The sphinx's lion body relaxes slightly, and it nods in a manner that is both regal and respectful.
>"Correct, travelers," it intones, its voice a resonant blend of ancient winds and echoing memories. "Your intellect and insight have proven your worthiness. You have earned the right to continue on your path, for the challenges that lie ahead require minds as sharp as yours."
>The tension that hung in the air like a suspended storm eases, and the ogres at the sphinx's side seem to relax their guard, their wary expressions giving way to a more neutral stance. It's as if the successful answers have not only secured your passage but also garnered a measure of respect from these guardians of the desert.
>"Proceed with your quest," the sphinx announces, its vulture head inclining in a respectful nod. "May your journey be as enlightening as the answers you provided. Remember that wisdom is often your greatest ally."
>As the Works in Progress steps forward, the sphinx's gaze follows, the weight of its ancient knowledge lingering like an intangible blessing. With each step further into the desert's embrace, you carry with you not only the satisfaction of overcoming its riddles but also the profound sense that this encounter was a testament to your collective wit and resourcefulness.*

### If the Party Does Not Solve All Three Riddles 

>*The sphinx's gaze remains unyielding as your response to the final riddle falls short of the mark. Its vulture eyes, once a blend of curiosity and assessment, now seem to pierce through you with an intensity that matches the blazing sun overhead. The very air seems to grow heavier, charged with the weight of a verdict yet to be pronounced.
"Your answers do not align with the truths I seek," the sphinx declares, its voice resonating with a note of disappointment that echoes like a distant lament. "You had the opportunity to prove your worthiness, yet you faltered at the precipice of wisdom."
The ogres, who had been standing at the sphinx's side like vigilant sentinels, suddenly shift their stance. Their expressions turn from passive observance to a readiness that speaks of impending conflict. The tension in the air is palpable, an electric charge that seems to herald the possibility of confrontation.
"By failing this trial, you have chosen a different path," the sphinx continues, its vulture head lowering slightly, a motion that is at once somber and final. "Now, the guardians that accompany me shall ensure that you do not proceed further. May you face these challenges with the same determination that brought you to this moment."*

## Notes
---
The sphinx made this portion of the Sands its home. It is accompanied by two rock chewer ogres, who apparently revere the sphinx.
