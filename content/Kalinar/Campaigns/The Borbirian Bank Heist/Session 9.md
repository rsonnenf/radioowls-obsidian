## Summary of Last Session
---
 ![[Session 9 approaching hut.jpg|right|700]][[Barnaby Baffletop|Barnaby]] shoots [[Kalinar/Notable People/Moose|Moose]] out of the trap he was stuck in. The commotion caused several animals to see what was going on. A fox stays around. Zos begins to talk with it and learns that people use the trap to catch food. She feeds it a goodberry, and another fox showed up for food. [[Zos]] asked if the foxes could lead us to the people who set the trap. They agree if we agreed to feed them. Moose remembered that he had a full ham on him, and he threw it for them. They started leading us to the people who set the trap, and after 30 minutes of walking, we find a hut. As we continue investigating, the foxes begin to follow us suspiciously. We determine through Barnaby's magic that there's something not natural about them. They begin to attack. Upon hitting them, they revert into men wearing red leathers. They wear an insignia based upon the [[Red Thorn]], but the insignia is torn through with claw marks. We determine that these are members of the Crimson Fang. After besting them, we attempt to interrogate them. We learn they are not druids and get a hint of shame as they talk to us. We let one of them go at the request of the other. Moose realizes that some of the splits of the Red Thorn were not always due to ideology, but sometimes due to the humanity of other groups. Sometimes they were cast out because they were not accepted as werewolves/werecreatures or warlocks who made a pact with a god the Red Thorn did not approve of. The remaining individual notes that he knows of the Wishing Well and hopes to be cured of his curse. He says his group was cursed in the same way. He acknowledges that the Crimson Fang has captured a boatful of scholars. He says if we wait a week, they'll be released from Timberfang Hideaway. But he says we can become one of the Crimson Fang to gain entry if we participate in the Great Hunt. We have to turn the moon red and get bitten. Or we can wait a week. We decide to wait.
