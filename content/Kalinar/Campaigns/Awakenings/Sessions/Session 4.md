## Summary of Last Session
---
 ![[Pasted image 20240611210318.png|right|700]]After waking on a beach, you find footprints leading into the forest until you come across [[Wir]], a dwarf, who is a member of a group of escapees from Fort Gloomwind. You accompanied Wir to the group's hideout, where you meet with their leader, Allana. [[Allana]] explained that they attempted to escape through a cave system they dug into, but the authorities were somehow aware of their escape attempt. The authorities pursued them, and one of their group, a man named [[Christopher]], held them off while the rest made it out. Allana and the group are making plans to rescue Christopher, and you agree to help. First, you meet with the group's Contact, an aide of [[Administrator Haera]]. He gives you a map marking the location of a weapons cache and a map of Gloomwind showing where Christopher is likely held. Gloomwind authorities cut the meeting short by attempting to capture you all, and during the fight that ensues, the Contact escapes.


## Notes
---
Weapons Cache

You make your way to the weapons cache as indicated on the map given to you by the contact. Again, the way is slow going, but about an hour later, you come across what you're looking for. Remaining hidden in the brush, you look out to see a large bluff. Scouting around, you notice that carved into the bluff on the south side is a large stone doorway, and several guards are posted out front guarding the chamber inside. On the eastern side, you notice a natural gap about three feet by three feet that appears to provide passage into the bluff itself.

Occurrences Inside

- Must find way to unlock vault –

- The door is sentient.

- When you touch the massive door, the polished stone begins to ripple, and slowly out of the face of the vault door, the stone face of a dragon materializes. "Who goes there?" asks the face.
- Door guards tomb of Rhovroth Tharnin. She was an honorable dragonborn warrior who perished 400 years ago.
- If players ask to be let in, dragon responds, "This sacred place has been defiled enough by these brutes. They broke me open and sullied that which I was entrusted to guard before sealing me again. Why should I grant you passage?"

- Persuasion DC 15 to convince to open. Persuasion DC 10 if part of reasoning is the players killed guards/will take the weapons from inside.
- On success: Pausing to think, the door mulls over what you've said. After some time, he says, "I have already failed in my task to protect that which I was entrusted to guard. Perhaps my failure may be redressed by allowing you to remove the uninvited and disgraced items within." The door pauses, considering something. "Before I allow you entry, you must answer three questions." [PLAY FARHAR]

- What is the color of the sky on a crisp spring morning? [after answer] "Ah. While I have always been proud to guard that which was entrusted to me, neither my duty nor my being has allowed me to observe the earthly wonders that exist outside of this vault." A droplet of water materializes out of the corner of the dragon's eye.
- What is the smell of the trees that blow in the wind? [after answer] "How wondrous. I yearn to smell them one day." He breathes heavily, clearly moved by this beauty he has never experienced.
- How beautiful is the sight of the sunset as one overlooks a vast field from atop the highest mountain? [after answer] After a moment of silence, you see several more droplets of water leaking out of the door's eyes. He says, "Thank you, my friends. Perhaps one day I might experience these wonders." The face briefly retreats back into the door, and a crevice materializes down the middle of it. The doors swing open inward, into the tomb. When they stop moving, the face materializes once more, on the right-half portion of the opened doors. "Thank you for ridding me of these constant reminders of my failure."

- Door face can be removed, if players ask. They must break off a piece of the door; the sentient essence will travel into that broken portion and can be taken by players. If done so, Door will later suggest being fashioned into a piece of equipment to assist the players as it travels with them.
