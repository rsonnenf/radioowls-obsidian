## Summary of Last Session
---
You entered the ice cavern of Frostwind Keep as Ulthurus, the white dragon eyed you down. You learned from him that he possessed the Horn of Valdune, and when [[Talia]] offered to trade him her idol she took from a Yeti's layer several weeks prior, Leo tried and successfully convinced Ulthurus that the statue was incredibly valuable. He tried to take it, but as he tried to do so, Orsik sent Ulthurus to another realm for one minute.  In the midst of the minute, you took the horn that was on Ulthurus's treasure pile and killed the kobold who was standing near Ulthurus and the wolfpack leader and ran out of the cavern.  When Ulthurus returned to this plane, he began to chase you.  Things were looking dire until Leo polymorphed Ulthurus into a snail, and in the moment of quiet, the wolf/Vilyomah explained that he felt forced to ally with the kobolds and Uthurus to take back the wolves' land, which has been encroached upon by Mayor Stonehammer and his dwarven people.  He explained, though, that the arrangement changed from one of mutual benefit, in which he aided Ulthurus and Ulthurus would help the Vilyomah regain his land, to one in which the Vilyomah and the pack became servants to the dragon.  He led you out of the cave and met with two other wolves, and you convinced them to attempt negotiations with Stonehammer to come to a resolution with the border and land dispute.  You gave them the Horn as a bargaining chip, and the Vilyomah reluctantly, but gratefully agreed, thanking you for your assistance.  The Vilyomah told you that they would attempt negotiations after they returned to the pack and informed them of the plan, then left, and you all agreed to eliminate Ulthurus in order to prevent him from causing harm to the nearby town.  You reentered Frostwind Keep, found where he remained a snail, dropped the polymorph spell, and began a hard-fought battle.  After struggling with him for some time, you were able to slay the dragon, and you decided to reward yourselves by taking his treasure from his lair, which included a vast amount of gold and gems.
