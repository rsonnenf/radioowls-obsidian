## Summary of Last Session
---
 You all woke up in cells on a boat. When a guard went to check on Grum-Grum, he forced the guard into the cell and locked him in before freeing the rest of the group. The guard told them that the ship is on its way to Fort Gloomwind. They spoke with Thea, who asked the group to retrieve a medallion, given to her by her sister, from the Captain, who took it from her. They also met [[Rilad]], who told them about an equipment room on the ship. The group went to the room and overcame a guard and guard dog before equipping themselves with weapons and armor. Afterwards, Grum-Grum bashed open Rilad's cell, causing several guards to come running to see what the noise was. After an intense battle, Troy ended the conflict by cleaving one of the guards in two. Rilad and another prisoner, who left the cell to help after retrieving some shortbows, are disturbed by the scene. [[Rilad]] looks ready to angrily approach the group to chastise them for making so much noise breaking them out.

### Top Deck

As you walk up the steps to the main deck, you’re briefly blinded by the sunlight. You smell salty air and hear the sound of waves crashing against the sides of the ship down below. You hear several  individuals going about their work including conducting repairs and mopping the deck.

Perception DC 10 - In front of you, you see several lifeboats on the deck. They’re cradled in release mechanisms in the event they need to be launched due to an emergency. Looking behind you as you come up the steps, you see a door to a large enclosed room. That is definitely the captain’s quarters. Atop the captain’s quarters, you see the ship's steering wheel manned by a helmsman. 

Perception DC 15 - You notice five guards and see that a few of the guards are standing fairly close to the lifeboats. They’ll likely see you if you try to launch one of them.

When you make your way into the cabin, you see a long table cluttered with papers, maps, and trinkets. All manner of items are strewn about the room. The bay windows provide some light and give a beautiful view of the sea. In front of the widows sits a man. He meets your eyes, and you notice that he doesn't appear to be surprised that you've escaped, but you catch the slightest impression that for some reason beyond the circumstances of your escape he fears you. "Your insurrection is at an end," he says, almost whispering. "You've nowhere to go." He stands from his chair and draws his sword. "You have questions, no doubt. But Gloomwind draws near. And if you live long enough to make it there, some of those questions may be answered."

- Captain Seifer Whitmore will explain:

- He and his crew accepted a contract from a government official to round up traitors to the realm of Kalendel. He won't explain who the official was because it is confidential.
- The rumors are true. Fort Gloomwind is a prison camp and self-contained city. It houses people who are "threats," but what the criteria are for what constitutes a "threat" is unknown to him.
- The Fort is managed by Administrator Haera. He has heard rumors that she possesses some sort of dark power, but he is uncertain whether that is true.  
     

As you make your attempt, you notice that, seemingly out of nowhere, the sunny, cloudless sky is overtaken by storm clouds in all directions. Rain begins to pour out of the sky, and the waves of the sea grow and begin to violently rock the ship...While the ship continues to rock, you see several massive tentacles emerge from the violent sea. They wrap around the ship, and the wood of the deck begins to buckle as the tentacles apply crushing pressure.

You watch as the tentacles of whatever the monstrosity is crush the deck, splitting the ship in two. You're horrified as the force of the monster and the violence of the storm catapult you from the ship and into the water. You begin to sink, and the darkness and depth of the sea takes you.
