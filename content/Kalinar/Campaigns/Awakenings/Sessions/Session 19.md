## Summary of Last Session
---
After tying up loose ends, including obtaining a map, at [[Courdney]], you set out on your way to Twindolen to assist against an Imperial army and to meet with Evalyn, a [[Justicars|Justicar]] serving Queen Jamil. After several days of travel through the mountain range abutting Courdney, you made your way into the Forell Forest, where you encountered a Treant, whom you have dubbed "[[RPG Rules/Pathfinder 2e/Mechanics/Backgrounds/Trailblazer|Trailblazer]]" since he cannot recall his name. He told you that the forest is cursed, and that he would lead you through it if you agreed to raze the part of the forest that has failed to "flourish" and help him regrow it. You agreed. He led you to a ravine, and while attempting to cross it, [[Nailo]] and [[Talia]] fell in. After [[Grum-Grum]] made it across, he asked Trailblazer to assist them, and Trailblazer became visibly offended because assisting those who have failed is not "the will of the forest." After all of you make your way across, you see Trailblazer standing with his back toward you. Without looking at you, clearly still offended, he says, "I will next lead you to the Cauheim. A river that runs through the forest. We must cross it, and we will reach that part of the forest that has failed to flourish. We must cull it." After about an hour of trekking through the forest, following Trailblazer's lead, you arrive at a massive river about 200 feet wide.

- Once across, he says, "Now, we will raze that which does not provide." He leads you, quietly, about another 20 minutes through the forest until he comes to a sudden stop. He says, "We are here. This portion does not provide. It does not flourish. It must be destroyed to permit new growth."

- If players burn, treant begins to laugh maniacally. He is bound by the will of the forest, which does not permit him to destroy any part of it. But destroy the forest, he must. Perception DC 15 to notice that the forest appears quite lush; lots of green; lots of creatures. Perception DC 15 to see that he has led you to a particularly dense portion of the forest. You discern that, if you don't do something about the fire, it will cause untold destruction to a large portion of the forest, more than you expected.
