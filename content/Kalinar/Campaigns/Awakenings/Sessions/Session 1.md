## Summary
---
 The players wake in a ship’s brig without knowing how they got there or where they’re going. When they escape, they encounter a half-elf named Rilad who explains that none of the captives know why they were taken and notes that for some reason, the players were kept separate from the rest of the captives. She tells the players to find a way to escape. Before they are successful, however, a storm rapidly appears and sinks the ship.
### In their Cells

A forceful jolt awakens you. As you gain your bearings, you look around the room. It's small. You're lying on an uncomfortable bed, and there doesn't appear to be anything else in the room. The entryway to the room is barred. This is very clearly some sort of cell, but your mind is foggy, and you can't recall how you got here, where you are, or why you're here. As you look out through the bars, you see an armored man sitting at a table. He has a deck of cards arranged so that it looks like he's playing some sort of solo game.  
 

Persuasion DC 19 - Guard gives over keys to cell. You watch the guard mull over your request in his head. After a long moment, he takes the keys off his belt and discretely hands them to you. “I never much liked this contract life anyway. We used to be swashbucklers. Now look at us. Low-life mercenaries.”

Intimidation DC 17 to force guard to hand over keys.

Upon Escaping

Looking around, you see across the hall four adjacent cells. Like your own cells, they are barred.

Perception DC 8 – Party notices that the cells are filled with multiple people, unlike your own cells where they were the only ones. Most appear too exhausted to even notice your escape, but a few watch you intently.

### Cleaning out Lower Deck and Entering the Equipment Room

You enter the room and see two fully-armored men sitting in a chair at a table, asleep with what you suspect is a guard dog, also asleep . Looking around the room, you see all manner of equipment. Hand axes and greataxes, javelins, shortswords, shortbows and light crossbows, leather and chain mail armors. This must be the equipment room, and this man must be the bosun, in charge of all of it.
