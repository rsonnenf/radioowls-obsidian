## Description
---
![[Council Chamber.jpg|right|700]]The Hall of the Council is the meeting chamber of the [[The Council of Seven Hammers|Council of Seven Hammers]], where 

As you enter the council chamber, you are struck by the solemnity and grandeur of the space. The room is large, with high ceilings and stone walls carved with intricate patterns and designs. Torches mounted on the walls cast flickering light across the room, illuminating a long table in the center of the chamber.

The table is made of polished wood, and is surrounded by seven high-backed chairs, each one ornately carved with dwarven symbols and motifs. At the head of the table sits the chair of the council leader, its backrest towering high above the others.

Along the walls of the chamber, there are shelves and cabinets filled with scrolls, books, and ancient tomes. These are the records of dwarven history, detailing the exploits of heroes long gone and the deeds of great leaders who have guided the dwarves through the ages.

The floor of the chamber is made of polished stone, and a large rug woven with intricate patterns and designs covers its center. In the middle of the rug, there is a large dwarven emblem, marking this as the council's sacred space.

The room is filled with a sense of reverence and gravity, as if the very air is charged with the weight of dwarven history and tradition.

The council chamber is a place of decision-making, where the most important matters of state are discussed and debated. It is a space that has witnessed the rise and fall of kings, the forging of alliances, and the resolution of great conflicts. It is a place of power and authority, a symbol of the dwarven people's resilience and strength.
