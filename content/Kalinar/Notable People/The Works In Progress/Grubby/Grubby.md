## Backstory
___
It was supposed to be a quiet day in the little village of ((TOWN)). It’s a little farming community where nothing bad ever happened. Other than maybe the occasional vole infestation, or high winds blowing over the corn stalks. Otherwise, life in ((TOWN)) was said to be perfect. Except for maybe a few scattered ruffians.

“Thank you Master Bellok. I’ll make sure you don’t have to deal with this again. I’ll give Kreygug a stern talking to. I appreciate you bringing him back here, and I apologize for the trouble he’s causing,” Akri said.

“Not a problem, ma’am. Just be aware… I’ve been guarding (TOWN) for quite a few decades now, and I gotta say. Kids like Kreyg there grow up to become trouble if you don’t nip that behavior in the bud,” Master Bellok said.

“I understand. It won’t happen again.”

“See to it that it doesn’t.”

The door shut slowly, creaking. Akri let out a long sigh, and little Grubb’rhun being held in his mothers arms could have sworn that he saw a puff of smoke and fire breathe out of his mother’s red nostrils. “Run along, dear. Mama has to speak with your brother.”

And so little Grubb’rhun Orbash was set down on the hardwood floor while he hobbled away pretending to blow fire out of his mouth like he definitely just saw his mother do. Meanwhile, Akri knocked twice on Kreygug’s door and, without even waiting for a reply, pushed the door open and invited herself in.

Grubb’rhun always hated this part. Though he was only about a year old and still a young child, he would be going to school soon and at least knew right from wrong. Happy from mad. Grubb’rhun always opted for happy.

The voices muffled through the doorway, and Grubb’rhun could hear his mother yelling at his brother from behind the door. “Well if Atkola hopped into a bag of holding, would you hop in, too!? Kreygug, for gods’ sake, your five. You’re going to be an adult next year. You can’t keep going off and causing chaos! I keep telling you that Atkola is trouble. He’s always getting you in trouble! You’re smarter than that! Your father and I didn’t raise you to be like this! Master Bellock said that you bought liquor for broodlings and tried to pick a fight! Where did you even get the money to buy that!?”

Grubb’rhun knew where the money came from. Grubb’rhun knows that his mother has precisely 4 delicate gold chain necklaces with exactly 328 golden links in total between the four of them… But one has gone missing. Only 246 shiny golden chainlinks remain. Grubb’rhun had considered many many times to take one of those necklaces for himself, but he knew that would make his Mama angry, and Grubb’rhun always opted for happy. Kreygug on the other hand…

Eventually, Holpo, Grubb’rhun and Kreygug’s father, returned home. Holpo was a larger green skinned kobold who worked pest control at one of the farms in town. Not glamorous work, but good enough to keep a roof over the heads of the Orbash family. Kreygug inherited his father’s physique, and Grubb’rhun inherited his mother’s brain. 

More fighting. More yelling. Lots of “this is the last straw,” and “is this what you want to become,” and “you need to turn your life around” was coming from the next room over. Meanwhile, Grubb’rhun was playing Dungeons and Dragons. It was his and his Papa’s favorite game. Grubb’rhun would be the dragon, of course, while Holpo would move the weak little wooden adventurer toys into Grubb’rhun’s dungeon to steal his fantastic treasure hoard of 3 copper pieces and a single silver.

“I am Grubb’rhun, the Emerald Beguiler, the Noxious Night Terror, the Venomous Tyrant! Bow before me or face death!!” And with that, Grubb’rhun took in a mighty breath and blew over only one of the three little adventurer figures before knocking the other two over with his little taloned hands. One day, his breath weapon will grow in. And it will definitely be poison like his green scale color dictates. He already has the bad breath to go with it.

After Grubb’rhun destroyed the trespassing treasure hunters, he heard a door slam open and stomping. Grubb’rhun cracked open his door to see his brother, Kreygug, walking toward the front door with a pack on his back. Kreygug turned back seeing his baby brother in the doorway, looked him right in the eyes and said, “See you later, Grubby. Don’t let Mom and Pop control you for your whole life. There’s a big world out there. Come find me when you’re old enough.”

That was the last Grubb’rhun ever saw of Kreygug. And the first time Grubb’rhun ever felt a bit of sad emptiness. But! There’s always a shiny silver lining. Kreygug was going to see the big wide world! And Grubb’rhun was going to get to meet him out there again someday. Maybe Kreygug would call a large fort his own, and have a dragon hoard of his own. That way, he wouldn’t have to keep stealing from Mama.

Life after Kreygug left was different. Akri and Holpo started watching Grubb’rhun so closely. And they would always critique the littlest mistakes Grubb’rhun would make. Or they would try to control his life choices. Akri and Holpo always used to ask little Grubb’rhun, “What do you want to be when you grow up?” And Grubb’rhun’s answer was always the same: “A DRAGON!!” Akri and Holpo always used to laugh at that and scream in terror at Grubb’rhun’s might, but now, they looked unamused by the answer and even once asked Grubb’rhun, “That’s nice, but have you ever thought about being an accountant? You’re so good with numbers.”

Over the next four years of schooling, Akri and Holpo pushed Grubb’rhun extremely hard to the point where Grubb’rhun really had no time for friends or hobbies. Just his studies. In fact, his closest friends were two other kobolds in his teaching cohort: Mutru and Rukla.

Mutru and Grubb’rhun met in Grubb’rhun’s second of five years of schooling in their band course. Mutru and Grubb’rhun had both picked up the pan flute as their instrument of choice. Grubb’rhun wasn’t bad at the pan flute, per se. His parents wouldn’t allow him to be bad because they forced him to practice at least an hour each day in order to get their instrument rental money’s worth. Through Grubb’rhun was decent, Mutru was far better. 

Rukla and Grubb’rhun met in their Draconic history course where they learned the ancient language of the dragons as well as mythological stories of ancient dragons. Grubb’rhun would read about Cyan Bloodbane getting his tail scale stolen or about the rule of Ashardalon, the extraplanar dragon. Rukla was always far more interested in the etymology of the language or the history of the stories. Grubb’rhun just imagined himself being those ancient dragons of old. On more than one occasion, Grubb’rhun’s teachers would call him out for absent mindedly juggling his pencils in one hand instead of actually listening.

And then there were Snem and Tukbi. Those two treated Grubb’rhun awfully, but it was just because they were upset that they weren’t going to grow up into real dragons like Grubb’rhun was going to, so it was OK that they were being mean because they’re just taking out their anger on someone who can take it. Snem and Tukbi were a year older than Grubb’rhun, but they were in the same broodling cohort because they were deemed unfit to move on after their first year of formal education. They didn’t hesitate to use their extra bulk to push the younger broodlings around.

The formal schooling continued, and now five years old and in his final year of schooling, Grubb’rhun’s parents sat him down for a serious conversation.

“Grubb’rhun. This is your last year at school. Have you thought about what you want to do when you graduate?”

“Yeah, I’m going to be a dragon.”

Holpo hid his face in his palms at this answer.

“OK, Grubb’rhun. No more joking. You know you’re not going to be a dragon--”

“Yeah, I am. I’m going to grow up and become the Emerald Beguiler! The Noxious Night Terror!!”

Holpo took his face out of his hands at this. “Grubb’rhun!” he yelled. “You need to stop this dragon nonsense. It’s not even physically possible! We’re kobolds, not dragons! I don’t know where you got in your mind that you’re going to grow up into this giant being, but it needs to stop. Your almost six. You’re almost an adult. You need to start acting like one.”

This cut Grubb’rhun deeply, but he refused to let it show. His parent’s rudeness was just them trying to do what they think is best for him. But he’s still going to become a dragon. No questions asked. 

“We’ve thought about getting you enrolled in the ((MAGE COLLEGE)) in Twindolen. That way, you can stick with your friends Mutru and Rukla. Mutru already got accepted in the ((BARD COLLEGE)) and Rukla is going there to become a linguist. We’ve already spoken to their parents, and they like the idea. We have just enough money to pay for the application fee. We will have to take out some loans to pay for travel, and you’ll split room and board between Mutru and Rukla. You’ll have to apply for scholarships to cover the cost of tuition, and you’ll need to--”

Holpo’s voice trailed off in Grubb’rhun’s head. He wasn’t listening anymore. Going to ((MAGE COLLEGE)) was out of the question. Dragons don’t go to college. They go to cities. They steal princesses! They find keeps and castles and dungeons! They find treasure! Not school! Shiny things!

Holpo kept explaining exactly what Grubb’rhun was going to do, and Grubb’rhun just kept nodding absentmindedly. Eventually, the laundry list of tasks, chores, applications, homeworks and more ended, and Grubb’rhun left to go for a walk.

Outside, he walked around aimlessly, overwhelmed by his parents' demands. Overwhelmed with the idea of even more schooling. Of being stuck behind the walls of a large city. Grubb’rhun hated the idea, and he almost wished that he had left with Kreygug almost five years ago to join him on his nomadic journey. “I wonder where Kreygug is now… Probably the leader of some group of misfits. I can’t wait to see him again. It’s been too long.”

Plastered to the wall of the Dragon’s Hoard, the local general store, Grubb’rhun found a fantastical looking poster adorned with bright colors, wicked artwork, and flashy text. 

  

COMING NEXT WEEK TO ((TOWN))!! THE DUKE AND DUCHESS’S TRAVELING CIRCUS OF WONDER AND WHIMSY

  

“A travelling circus, huh? And the week before graduation? Should be fun. Maybe I can convince Mutru and Rukla to go with me.” Grubb’rhun gathered his dragon hoard up that week. He managed to find enough for a single ticket, and maybe a couple of games or attractions, and he was so glad that he did.

Grubb’rhun, Mutru, and Rukla managed to sneak a couple of drinks from one of the liquor tents. They had enough to buy one drink, each, which would be enough to get them drunk enough given their small stature. Mutru opted to buy a drink called the Pixie Piss while Rukla bought the Bogwater Brew. A different drink caught Grubb’rhun’s mind--the Dragonfire. It was a small shot that tasted of spicy chilies and orange rind, and after taking the small drink, Grubb’rhun could feel the warmth spread throughout his entire body as his vision went slightly blurry and his gait started to sway back and forth.

The Circus was one of the best sights that Grubb’rhun had ever seen. Bards far more skilled than Mutru walked through charming the crowds. Archery contests. Tests of strength. A sleight of hand artist swindled people out of their money by deftly moving a pea from beneath one shell to another unseen. There was the Mud Pit which involved two men and a woman putting on a spectacularly absurd show about how she was going to be thrown into a volcano by one of the men, but the other man came to rescue her. It always ended with all three of them rolling in the mud. Very messy, but very funny. But by far, Grubb’rhun was most interested in Dracana Ignis, the dragon-looking woman who danced with and breathed fire. 

“See! She’s almost a dragon already! She can even breath fire! She just needs to grow her wings,” Grubb’rhun excitedly told Mutru and Rukla, who once again gave him blank stares. He knew what they were thinking. “YoU’rE nOt GoInG tO gRoW uP iNtO a DrAgOn, GrUbB’rHuN.” Yeah, whatever.

“Still thinking you’re gonna grow up into a dragon, Grub?!” It was Snem with Tukbi close behind. “What makes you think you’re ever gonna grow up to be any more than you currently are: a little kobold worth less than the mud on my boots!” Mutru and Rukla started to back up, but damn the liquid courage of the Dragonfire. 

Grubb’rhun stepped forward. “My name is Grubb’rhun, the Emerald Beguiler, the Noxious Night Terror, the Venomous Tyrant! Bow before me or face dea--” At the climax of his honorifics, Grubb’rhun’s stomach lurched, and what went down was now quickly coming back up as he sprayed bile and circus food all over the fronts of Snem and Tukbi. They screamed in disgust as they tucked tail and ran. “Weird… I thought my breath weapon was supposed to be poison, not acid… Maybe I have some black dragon in my bloodline!”

The following week, Grubb’rhun, Mutru, and Rukla all graduated along with the rest of their cohort. The Duke and Duchess’s Traveling Circus of Wonder and Whimsy was packing up to leave, and the three were scheduled to be shipped off to Twindolen to start the next part of their education. This saddened Grubb’rhun because that overbearing thought of being stuck behind the city walls was constantly invading his mind. 

Then, the wild thought crossed Grubb’rhun’s mind. Why not just ship off with the circus? Surely he could find some kind of work with them. He was good with numbers, so he could balance the budgets of the vendors. Or keep pests out like his father did on the farm. He could do something. Anything! As long as it kept him away from permanently being stuck behind city walls and kept him moving on the road to explore the world and find his dragon cave.

And so, the night before he was meant to leave with Mutru and Rukla, Grubb’rhun wrote a note to his parents.

Mama and Papa. I’m off to grow up into a real dragon. There’s a big world out there. Bigger than Twindolen. And dragons need room to grow. I don’t think they would be allowed in the city. Maybe I’ll run into Kreygug while I’m out there. I’ll stop by when I’m big and fierce and the Emerald Beguiler. --Love, Grubb’rhun.

He slipped out of his window and ran toward the circus that was just leaving. Over the next weeks, Grubb’rhun managed to pick up odd jobs with the circus-folk. He met Duke Agarald Sinbrandt and Duchess Ellaria Sinbrandt, a husband and wife and not a Duke or Duchess at all--just the owners of this circus. He also met Rahstock Silverhand, the dwarven strongwoman, Archibold Devilsong, the tiefling bard, and of course, Dracana Ignis, the dragonborn firebreather. Grubb’rhun became close friends with Rahstock, Archibold, and Dracana, and over the next four years, at the old age of ten, Grubb’rhun had become a regular act in the circus.

Archibold would play accompanying tunes for Grubb’rhun the Emerald Beguiler as he juggled swords and daggers in the air, pulling them seemingly out of thin air. Rahstock would toss Grubb’rhun into the air like he weight nothing while the little kobold flipped and flopped through the air somehow managing to right himself before landing on his feet. And eventually, Dracana Ignis taught Grubb’rhun how to breathe fire through a bit of trickery by filling his mouth with a bit of alcohol and spitting it out. The audience whooped and hollered at the smallest “dragon” in existence. Little did they know that soon… SOON, Grubb’rhun would become a real dragon.

That is… If his life lasted long enough. Grubb’rhun found himself wandering the circus one day after they had set up outside the city of Hornsmead. An interesting sight caught his eye: an emerald dragonborn facing off against another opponent in an archery tournament. And this dragonborn’s coin purse was not securely attached to his belt. As the dragonborn pulled back the bowstring and aimed his final shot, Grubb’rhun snuck up behind and yanked at the coin purse which was more secure than Grubb’rhun expected. It didn’t come off of the belt, and the maneuver instead caused the dragonborn to notice the pickpocket and miss his shot.

Grubb’rhun turned tail and sprinted away, but the dragonborn saw him and gave chase. After a few minutes, Grubb’rhun was cornered by two other people he thought he would never see again: Snem and Tukbi with daggers out approaching Grubb’rhun. “See you’re not a dragon still, eh Grub!? We still haven’t forgiven you for that little stunt you pulled four years ago. Why don’t we just slit your throat so that you’ll never get to try out that breath weapon again, eh?”

Grubb’rhun was trapped. Snem and Tukbi were far too quick for him, and behind them, the emerald dragonborn was approaching. Grubb’rhun realized he was in deep shit. Snem pushed Grubb’rhun over while Tukbi held him down to the ground. The dragonborn yelled to the two bullies, “Wait! Let me have a go at him.” Snem and Tukbi cackled at the idea and stepped back, giving the dragonborn space to end Grubb’rhun’s life. The tall dragonborn stepped up and unhooded Grubb’rhun, finally seeing the kobold’s green face clearly, his eyes squinted preparing for the blow across his face.

But something unexpected happened. The dragonborn hesitated. He let go of Grubb’rhun’s cloak and turned on Snem and Tukbi, scaring them off. “Don’t you dare ever enter this circus again! Or I’ll break your miserable little kneecaps!” The dragonborn shouted before walking away.

Grubb’rhun slowly got up, shocked at what just happened. Grubb’rhun ran up to the dragonborn and reached toward the exposed coin purse again before second guessing himself. Grubb’rhun instead tugged on the dragonborn’s shirt. “Hey. Sorry for messing up your shot. I just… Sometimes I get these urges to hoard shiny things, and your coin purse looked like an easy target…”

“Hoard shiny things?” the dragonborn asked.

“Well, yeah. I’m going to become the greatest dragon that ever lived! I’m going to amass a massive hoard of coins and gems and magical artifacts. And… Well, your coin purse has some of the shinies I need, so I figured… But anyway, when I become rich and famous, and you hear the name of Grubb’rhun, the Emerald Beguiler, the Noxious Night Terror, the Venomous Tyrant, I’ll make sure to pay you back for saving me.

For ((INDETERMINATELY LONG AMOUNT OF TIME)) Fax Faetrin and Grubb’rhun “Grubby” Orbash partnered up in the Duke and Duchess’s Traveling Circus of Wonder and Whimsy. Archibold Devilsong would lure in a crowd and play music while Fast-Fletcher-Fax fired arrow after arrow toward the seemingly endless stream of daggers that Grubb’rhun the Emerald Beguiler would toss into the air. The two made a decent amount of coin during the day, and at night, Grubb’rhun would listen to Fax tell him stories of far off treasure and of great dragons.

By far, Grubb’rhun’s favorite story was about the Cyan Bloodband, the [[Orbs of Dragonkind|Orb of Dragonkind]], and the brewing of the first Potion of Dragon Mastery. “You remember the tales of Cyan Bloodbane, don’t you?” Fax would tell Grubb’rhun. “The cunning ancient green dragon who used the Orbs of Dragonkind? While they say that he had a dragonborn apprentice named Adrex the Aggressor. Though he was never able to unleash the dragon inside of the Orbs of Dragonkind himself, Adrex used the insight of Bloodbane to create the first Potion of Dragon Majesty. They say one night while Cyan was sleeping Adrex snuck into Cyan’s layer and stole a single scale off of the tip of his tail. Being suspicious of other dragons, Cyan called other great dragons to a meeting to see who had stolen his scale. Adrex used this opportunity to steal a scale from 10 different dragons. He spent years mixing these scales with different chemicals until he profected a potion that could turn anyone who drinks the potion into an adult dragon of the same color as the scale that was stolen.” A potion to help accelerate the growth into a dragon, AND an orb that would help release the dragon within??? These were extremely interesting to Grubb’rhun, and he knew that he had to have one if not both of them.

And so, time goes on. Fax and Grubb’rhun became the best of friends, and they’ve been inseparable. Fax always accompanied Grubb’rhun on his next great adventure at each of the towns as Grubb’rhun searched for the Potion of Dragon Majesty, the Orb of Dragonkind, or even any information about his long lost brother, Kreygug.
