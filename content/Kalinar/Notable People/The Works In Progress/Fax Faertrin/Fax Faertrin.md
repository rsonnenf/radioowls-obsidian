Bio:

*Warning-lots of made up information ahead. Rob, let me know what does and does not fit into your world.*

Heskan and Raiann had a lovely little cottage in the woods outside of Altium (the city with the snake people). Raiann would spend her morning’s hunting. When she returned she would join her husband and tend the livestock or help maintain their land. And at night they happily ate, drank and were happy. And then they were three.

Fax was an energetic child always having adventures with his loving parents. In the morning Fax would go with his mother and learn the lay of the lands. She would teach him how to follow the grass and twisted branches to track prey. After the two came home with their latest kill, Heskan would teach Fax how to help with the cattle on their ranch and how to fix the leaking roof of the cottage. And in the evenings, the three would be a happy family playing card games and telling stories by the fire light. 

“You know Fax, our people are sparse in these lands, but there are many lands where we are much larger in numbers. And just as we vary in different colors, we have as many personalities as the great dragons themselves.

“You remember the tales of Cyan Bloodbane, don’t you? The cunning ancient green dragon who used the [[Orbs of Dragonkind]]? While they say that he had a dragonborn apprentice named Adrex the Aggressor. Though he was never able to unleash the dragon inside of the Orbs of Dragonkind himself, Adrex used the insight of Bloodbane to create the first Potion of Dragon Majesty. They say one night while Cyan was sleeping Adrex snuck into Cyan’s layer and stole a single scale off of the tip of his tail. Being suspicious of other dragons, Cyan called other great dragons to a meeting to see who had stolen his scale. Adrex used this opportunity to steal a scale from 10 different dragons. He spent years mixing these scales with different chemicals until he profected a potion that could turn anyone who drinks the potion into an adult dragon of the same color as the scale that was stolen. (Cyan Bloodbane is a real dragon, Adrex is someone I made up)

“Or do you remember Fax, the Tamehearted, the ancient emerald dragon that you are named after? Well, I don’t think I have told you about Kiirith Delmiriv, a dragonborn just like you. Kiirith was a great warrior in these lands long long ago. The lands were in trouble because of a clan of ice giants were ravenging in the north. Determined to help, Kiirith traveled long and far to find Fax and ask him for help. Fax told him that he could not get involved in another war against giants and instead infused Fax’s bow with the power of his breath, forging a Dragon Wing Bow, and a set of dragon scale armor. With these kingly gifts Fax stormed the ice giants and slew them all in the name of Kalinar.” (Fax, the Tamehearted and Kiirith Delmiriv are made up)

“I can slay giants like him dad!” Fax would say.

“Oh, not now, its bed time now. But one day I know you will. You will save everyone, Fax. Now lets get to bed.”

“No dad, one more story please? How about the one about Thava!”

“Well, if you want to her about Thava, you’ll have to ask your mother.”

“Mom, tell the story of Thava please!”

“Alright, then straight to bed. Let me see… Ahh yes, Thava. Well, all of Kalinar was being tormented by Ashardalon the terrible multiplanar dragon. The king was desperate for help so he set out a contest of champions where the victor would be tasked with saving all of the realm. Amused by this effort, Ashardalon attended the contest himself and attacked all of the contestants the moment the affair had begun. As everyone ran away from the massacre Thava, a golden dragonborn, jumped to action and grabbing the champions’ prize from the king himself! It was the great Dragonlance that was a gift to the lands from Bahamut himself. She ran to the roof of the colosseum and leaped onto the back of Ashardalon and thrusted the pike under the scales between his wings. Impressed by the mortal, Ashardalon promised to leave the lands forever. Now go to bed.” (Thava is made up but Ashardalon is a real dragon)

Everything important to them was inside this house. 

Once a year Raiann would take a trip past Altium and to Twindlenn. Fax always cried when his mother would leave for the weeks-long journey, but when he was old enough to join he was eager for each summer to pass so they could travel in the fall. 

He was 13 years old when his mother allowed him to make the journey on his own. With his horse, a small cart, some trade goods and his trusty bow Fax embarked and enjoyed the time on his own, even though it was his first time ever being this alone. Fax enjoyed the journey as day after day he could zone out and enjoy the sound of the falling leaves crush below hoof and wheel. In fact he almost didn’t hear the cries for help because he was so absorbed in the wood around him. 

Fax snapped to and noticed a broken cart on the side of the road and the commotion coming from inside the woods. Pulling over Fax pulled his bow and snuck closer to get a better look. Through the brush Fax could see a woman being attacked by three strange men. Knowing he had to act, Fax pulled his bow and prepared for the perfect shot. After the snap of his first arrow, Fax ran to a new location and hid. Watching as one of the still breathing men tried to find the attacker, Fax knocked another arrow and took out the man still holding onto the woman. Realizing he was alone, the third attacker ran from the scene. 

Fax slowly approached the woman and asked if she was okay. When she turned towards him he could see her beautiful emerald scales and instantly fell in love. Her name was Biri Mystan, a dragonborn from the city of Twindlen. And in the upcoming days of their travels, they soon learned everything about each other.

Biri’s parents were merchants who traveled between Byseastyl and Twindlen. Biri was returning from a trip to Altium for her coming of age quest. The two laughed, shared stories of their upbringing, and enjoyed the week together in Twindlen. And when their time ended, Fax rode with Biri back to Byseastyl and then headed home, promising to see her next year.

And sure enough he did. For three straight years Fax would wait patiently through freezing winters, the laborious springs, and the hot summers just for his time with Biri. And on his last journey, at the age of 16, Fax didn’t return to the cottage but instead bought a home in Twindlen with his new wife Biri. 

Here Fax took up a post with the city guard and helped escort the citizens of Twindlen to far reaching lands (perhaps this is a way to connect Fax to other characters?). They lived a happy life, often visiting Biri’s family and even returning to Heskan and Raiann once a year. Fax was most excited to return to his parents cottage the year his son, Greroth, was born. 

It was the second year that Greroth was brought to Heskan and Raiann’s cottage that Greroth was brought on his first hunting trip. Three generations, two bows, and one kill. Perched on a ledge Fax held Greroth while Raiann lined the deer up with the notched arrow. The deer dropped before it knew what had hit her, Fax loved how his mother never missed. Greroth, seeing this for the first time, sprinted straight to the kill in pure joy and excitement. Just before reaching his destination Greroth tripped over a small branch and became covered in mud and maggots from a rotten pool. All three started laughing as Fax caught up to Greroth and as Raiann wiped off her grandson’s face she sung “Oh, my Grubby little boy.” and that became Greroth’s new nickname. 

The roads were becoming more dangerous. Fowl beasts and unsavory people made travel more difficult and Fax became busier than ever with his work. And he earned his way onto longer and more important guard missions. Once he helped a caravan of diplomats travelling from Twindlen to Theele. When beasts attacked Fax jumped on top of one carriage and slew several of them with his bow. As we stepped down he met a noble by the name of Fen. He had claimed he was going to change the world with his ideas and offered Fax a job. Fax refused not wanted to relocate his family, but they shared many stories on their journey to the capital. 

To help their son, Raiann and Heskan decided they would come to Twindlen. They were all excited to have the extra help and the fall couldn’t come soon enough. And yet it never did. Fall turned to winter, and winter to spring and the two never arrived. Fax had helped hundreds of travellers in his career, but he couldn’t help his parents. 

And that summer he took one last trip to the cottage. Helping the people of Twindlen meant nothing if he couldn’t help his family. Seeking more of his parents' guidance he moved his family back to the cottage to find his own retirement. For three years Fax would wake up, take his son hunting, help with livestock and the land, and then share the same stories to his son by the same fireplace he had heard them. 

Fax will always his first hunting trip with Greroth without his mother. They didn’t get a kill but that didn’t hinder Greroth’s mood. “Dad, I am going to be the greatest dragonborn hero of all times. I’m going to be just like Thava and jump on the back of evil dragons and slay them! Just you wait.” Fax just laughed. “Grubby, you will be great. I know it.” “And I’ll have my own pet drake one day too, just you wait.” “Oh, will you Grubby?” “Oh yes, see, I even found this dragon tooth when mom took me out for a walk last week.” Fax had known about this tooth for a while. Biri said he was so cute when he found it. Fax was happy.

But rumors were spreading of trouble in the lands. A knock on his cottage door was a strange sound as people rarely were out this far in the woods. Word was that a new emperor had taken the Throne, the new Emperor Fen. Emperor Fen was recruiting old guards from across the lands to help protect his new people. This was to be an age of peace. And Fax was needed. The new empire promised to protect for his family and all they asked was one year of service and loyalty. Fax agreed. He knew he could transport the new emperor’s clients across the lands to help establish order. 

Before leaving, Greroth begged his father not to leave. But after promising Greroth that one year of work means they can play every year after, Greroth let Fax leave. “Wait dad, take this! (handing the tooth). It will remind you of how when you return I will be an even greater dragon hunter!” And with tears in his eyes, Fax turned and left. 

Fax would never have admitted it, but he had missed the road. He had spent over a decade travelling across Kalinar every year. For all of his travels, he had learned more stories about the world around him and many languages from all of the wild people he met. But with the joy of the job, Fax missed his family. One year for a lifetime of peace. And then the news arrived. Biri was marked as a traitor for housing conspirators of the new empire. And the punishment for such a crime was imprisonment and then death. Fax received the message after the scheduled execution date. And left the people he was escorting alone in the middle of their journey. 

The city of Twindlen had taken his parents from him and the new emperor had taken his wife and child. He vowed to never leave his family alone again, but he had no family to make the promise to. And here Fax drank his sorrows away at the Whimsical Pig in Hornsmead. Years had passed taking up random jobs and gambling at the card games his parents had taught him to pay for drink in the small dwarvish settlement. Fax had gotten used to pointlessly going about day to day as long as the day ended in a black out. 

And then one day in summer a The Duke and Duchess’s Traveling Circus of Wonder and Whimsy had come to Hornsmead. Fax had heard they had exotic drink and figured he could gamble a few of the games to make a quick buck to pay for it all. An archery game to shoot filled waterskins as they were pulled across a line, easy. A quick shot game against thrown disks, child's play. He enjoyed the influx of people to the city that he could game against.

One day there was a traditional archery contest of varying distances. Knowing he would win, Fax entered as the dwarfs of Hornsmead were not known for their skill with a bow. Fax easily progressed to the final round against one other individual. He knew he had the match in the bag but just as he drew back his arrow he felt a tug at his coin purse and the distraction made the shot had gone wide. Looking down Fax saw a hooded individual running away and having just lost Fax sprinted after the riff raff who was ducking inbetween the tents and weaving easily through the crowds. 

After a few minutes of the chase Fax had thought he had lost the little one and was about to give up when he saw a hooded figure cornered by two others. Ready to beat the money and ale out of the one who made him lose, Fax ran closer. Apparently the two others were also ready to beat the snot out of the little shit and Fax was ready for some comradery. One pushed the hooded figure to the ground and the other picked him up to hold his arms back. Fax shouted out for them to stop and let him have his turn. The two laughed, stepped back, and allowed the tall dragonborn to step up. As a man who had lost his honor, Fax unhooded the figure and prepared to paint the walls with this hoodlum. 

And then Fax froze and sighed “Grubby?”  Was this Greroth? No. The ale must finally be getting to him. It was a little green kobold who looked at him the way his son did. And this wasn’t alcohol, this was a hard sobering reality. Fax once protected his wife and hundreds of other people. And now he was about to beat a child. His eyes filled with tears and his heart filled with rage. Not like this.

Fax pushed the hooded kid back softly and walked past the two aggressors. Angry and having saved the hooded individual, Fax walked away. Not 20 feet later he felt a tug on his shirt. “Hey. Sorry for messing up your shot. I just… Sometimes I get these urges to hoard shiny things, and your coin purse looked like an easy target…”

“Hoard shiny things?” the Fax asked.

“Well, yeah. I’m going to become the greatest dragon that ever lived! I’m going to amass a massive hoard of coins and gems and magical artifacts. And… Well, your coin purse has some of the shinies I need, so I figured… But anyway, when I become rich and famous, and you hear the name of Grubb’rhun, the Emerald Beguiler, the Noxious Night Terror, the Venomous Tyrant, I’ll make sure to pay you back for saving me.”

  The determination in his eyes reminded him of his sons just before a kill when they hunted. The two talked for hours and Fax found the naive optimism of Grubb’rhun a direct reminder of his happier days. And so Fax asked the carnival’s show master for a job. 

First, Fax was a laborer. Carrying goods, helping set up and take down the traveling show, and protecting his performers. One night a lousy bunch of drunks had come to the show to see the freaks. When the drunks stood up and threatened to stab one of the performers for being “unnatural” Fax quickly pulled out his bow and shot the dagger out of the aggressor's hand. The crowd went wild and the master of the show had a new act. (could be a backstory connection to have characters meet as someone tries to cause harm to the traveling circus, unrelated to this specific event) And that is how Fax got the nickname “Fast-Fletching-Fax

Fax spent time performing trick shots in between acts. And Fax got to spend more time with Grubb’rhun ([[Grubby]]). The two became inseparable. In every town Grubb’rhun would be in the search of a great adventure, and Fax would follow him. In the evenings Grubb’rhun would ask to hear stories of far off treasure or of great dragons. And Fax shared all of his favorite stories. Just like his dad did, and just like Fax had for his son. 

The life in a travelling circus made Fax happy. Seeing the world, having a purpose that didn’t serve a political purpose, and sharing time with outcasts like Fax made at home once more. One night Fax tried learning how to “firebreath” like Dracana Ignis did in the circus. He thought so hard about spitting the alcohol just right but couldn’t get it. She could make the flames dance and twirl like a dragon itself. Fax continued trying until he broke down remembering his son’s dream of having his own dragon. And so he gave up, no longer wasting the alcohol he was spitting, and sat alone until he was nudged on his arm. 

“Grubby, not now.” Another nudge. “Grubby… not… now!” As the next nudge came Fax smacked his hand back hard to find it hit a much more sturdy figure than his kobold friend. Instead, to his surprise was a fire drake trying to give him comfort. And he had a new familiar, who he named after his mom’s favorite hero, Thava.

Though Fax didn’t always approve of Grubby’s habit of stealing,Fax understood the desire to make one's dreams come true. Oftentimes, during his own performances, Fax would notice Grubby pickpocketing the crowd. One night, halfway through his show, Fax noticed Grubby being careless in his pickpocketing. Fax tried to up his game and distract the crowd more. Under the pressure, Fax pulled his bow string a little too far and the crowd gasped as the string snapped in two. This was when Grubby was caught stealing from Lord Kalrex himself. Fax helped Grubby escape and the two knew they would never be allowed back into the circus, or that town again without Grubbs facing death or imprisonment.

Description:

In a crowd you would never notice Fax Faertrin. Fax does stand a little taller than the average humanoid but not by much. 6’2” and 200 lbs does not get you noticed and neither does his green emerald like skin. He has a horn standing above each temple that look normal, but upon closer inspection one would notice they are made from broken emeralds that are held together by his psionic energy. Even when his hood is over the top of his head, the horns still float above unless he puts in an actual effort to conceal them. Fax wears dark brown leather armor under his dark grey cloak. His left hip is adorned with a shortsword while his right hip a sheath for his dagger. Hanging from his back is a dark brown quiver full of arrows and a left handed Bow, for Fax fights right handed in melee and left handed at range. Uniquely hanging just below his scaly jaw is a leather necklace with a tooth like stone hanging from it. 

If you see Fax by himself, you either are being robbed or you need to stay clear. Almost always Fax is accompanied by the talkative and sticky-fingered Grubby, his kobold in crime, or Thava, his small pet drake that keeps him comfort even on his darkest days.**
