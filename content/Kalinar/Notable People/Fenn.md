## Overview
---
An Emperor of Kalinar who took the throne by force. Thirty years ago before the Awakening, [[Brondur]]'s heir, Fenn, became dissatisfied with his advisory position as Counselor and sought to become Emperor. He arranged a coup and overthrew then-Empress Hayden, seizing power for himself and purging the [[Justicars]] during the course of the 5-year War of Ash between loyalists to Empress Hayden and Fenn's supporters. Upon seizing control, Fenn executed Hayden and implemented an oppressive rule to keep dissidents in check.  A small band of resistance grew throughout the realm seeking to oppose Fenn's tyrannical rule.


Fenn, the current Emperor of Kalinar, casts a long shadow over the realm. Unlike [[Emperor Dalran|Dalran]] and Brondur, his legacy is not one of unification and liberation, but of ambition and ruthless power grabs.

## **A Discontented Heir**
---
Fenn, heir to the position of Counselor under Empress Hayden, chafed at the limitations of his advisory role. Consumed by ambition, he desired ultimate power.

## **Seeds of Betrayal**
---
Fenn's discontentment festered. He orchestrated a cunning coup, overthrowing Empress Hayden in a five-year conflict known as the War of Ash. This bloody conflict pitted loyalists to the Empress against Fenn's supporters, fracturing the once-unified Kalinar.

## **Iron Fist Rule**
---
Having seized the throne, Fenn transformed Kalinar into a repressive state. To quell dissent and maintain control, he instituted harsh policies and purged the [[Justicars]], the guardians of justice who might have challenged his rule. Fenn's reign stands as a stark contrast to the era of peace and unity ushered in by Dalran and Brondur.

## **A Legacy of Oppression**
---
Fenn's ruthless ambition shattered the ideals upon which the Kalinarian Empire was built. His reign is a dark chapter, marked by oppression and a stifling of dissent. The rise of rebel groups across Kalinar signifies a growing resistance against his tyranny. Whether Fenn can quell this resistance or be overthrown remains to be seen, but his legacy as an emperor who prioritized personal power over the well-being of his people seems certain.
